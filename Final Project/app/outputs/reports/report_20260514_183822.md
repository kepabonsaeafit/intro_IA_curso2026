# Question under investigation

The debate of LLMs vs traditional ML revolves around their relative strengths and weaknesses in different scenarios. This comparative analysis aims to identify scenarios where LLMs outperform traditional ML and where they underperform.

## Pro-LLMs (LLM hype)

LLMs, which leverage large transformer-based architectures, have gained significant attention for their ability to excel in NLP tasks and adapt to large volumes of unstructured data. Their key strengths are:

1. **Emerging capabilities**: LLMs demonstrate emergent abilities, such as in-context learning and multimodal reasoning, which have not been explicitly programmed into the models, yet can be fine-tuned for specific tasks.
2. **Zero-shot and few-shot learning**: LLMs excel in scenarios requiring little or no data, effectively leveraging their capacity to generalize from patterns in data.
3. **Multidisciplinary capabilities**: LLMs can understand and generate languages with different complexities and structures, making them valuable for a wide range of applications.

However, LLMs do have certain limitations, such as:

1. **Interpretability limitations**: LLMs often lack the interpretability of traditional models, which can be a critical aspect in certain domains, such as healthcare or finance.
2. **Performance gap for specific scenarios**: Despite making analytics accessible and understandable, LLMs underperform traditional models in specific scenarios, such as when the amount of data is limited or when interpretability and accuracy are equally important.

## Pro-ML traditional (Conservative perspective)

Traditional ML models, often based on algorithms like random forests, XGBoost, or support vector machines, have been considered more reliable in specific domains and tasks due to their interpretability and accuracy. Traditional ML subsets, such as ensemble learning and boosting, have surpassed LLMs in reliability and accuracy in various scenarios, including text classification and supervised learning tasks. While both LLMs and traditional ML models possess unique capabilities, it is crucial to recognize their different strengths and weaknesses.
